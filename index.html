Course Highlights:

There are few things I enjoy more than stretching my creative muscles. However, never completing any formal graphic design or art based courses before, learning the visual frameworks involved in this course forced me to view the world around me from a brand new perspective. Experimenting with the Cindy Brewer's ColorBrewer 2.0 was particularly interesting, and has become a go-to reference when designing and selecting colour palettes.

Yearning to explore a northern Ontario community that I have never been to, I focused my technical labs on the city of Whitehorse, Yukon. My favourite of these products being a 1:50,000 topographic map.

I was enjoy building my final project, in which I produced a 1:5,000 scale map illustrating the affect of structures damaged in the Nord-Kivu province of the Democratic Republic of Congo, resulting from the Mt. Nyrigagongo eruption in 2021. This was also a great experience as it allowed me to link my current studies back to my master's program studying Disaster & Emergency Management, when I did a deep dive on an earlier eruption of the same volcano.

A full size pdf of both the Yukon and the Mt. Nyrigagongo map are both available as full size PDFs in the GitHub repository.

Key Project Details:

● Produced a 1:5,000, 22 x 36' scale map illustrating the impacts of the 2021 Nyrigagongo, focused on the city perimeter of Goma in the Nord-Kivu province

● The intended audience for the map would be emergency management services and local community members, which is why a large map scale was selected

● A focal element is a generalized lava flow map, and affected building structure. These products were derived from data made available through UNITAR-UNOSAT and Sentinel-1 satellite imagery

● Due to the nature of the hazard, contour lines were emphasized in 5 meter detail.

● A 1:200,000 scale inset map with a local hillshade and the entire extent of the lava flow helps provide additional spatial context for the viewer

● Secondary data elements include existing roads and buildings

Despite my dreams to become a video game programmer in eight grade, I had no practical experience with any programming languages before beginning this program. Key Project Details:

● The resulting information is packaged into a feature class, a shapefile, and added to an ArcGIS Pro project

● This project incorporates ArcPy, as well as other python concepts learned over the semester

● Sunlight hour calculations are accurate within 30 minutes each day, with variance influenced by latitude. Optimal apartment height is based on receiving at least one hour of sunlight in a day.

Spatial Analysis I

Grade achieved:

96%

Course Competencies:

● Compare and select the most appropriate data model and GIS analysis method for a given problem at an introductory level

● Describe and perform basic GIS analysis with a variety of different data models using appropriate geoprocessing techniques

● Develop work flows to accomplish specific objectives and tasks at an introductory level

● Apply geoprocessing techniques using a model builder to solve spatial problems

● Effectively perform and complete a Spatial Analysis real world project scenario from data acquisition to pre-processing to analysis and presentation

● Using professional and technical language of GIS describe how spatial analysis techniques can be used in industry

Course Highlights:

Few things feel as rewarding as successfully solving a problem. That is why each spatial analysis technique that I add to my professional toolbox makes me feel as though I'm walking a bit taller.

Site suitability analyses were particularly rewarding experiences (cottage site suitability, wildlife habitat suitability, etc.), but the standout experience of this course for me was building multi-criteria decision analysis to conduct a spatial based risk assessment. Due to my background in emergency management and business continuity, I am no stranger to risk analysis. However, I have never had the opportunity to use GIS technology to incorporate spatial factors into into my criteria weighting. Prior to beginning the program, this was the type of projects I envisioned myself working on.

This course was also my introduction to Esri's Model Builder functionality. Leveraging this toolset redefined how I use ArcGIS Pro, and has been beneficial in nearly every ArcGIS project I have completed since.

Key Project Details:

● Risk assessment of a proxy pipe network in Whitehorse, Yukon

● Analysis criteria included water distribution networks, transportation features, key social infrastructure, landuse, unstable environments, and attributes of the pipe network itself

● Input data was collected, analyzed and prepared over the course of the semester

● Risk was calculated as (likelihood * vulnerability * resilience * maximum consequence), whose values were calculated based on spatial assessments of the various analysis criteria.

● This was a professor-led assignment, applied to a unique study area

Course Highlights:

In this condensed course we experimenting with spatial interpolation to model a Digital Elevation Map (DEM) and DEM derivatives, and building a hydrology model in ArcHydro using Ontario's Enhanced Flow Direction grid. Both projects are highlights of the entire program. It is a shame this course can't go on even longer, because environmental modelling is a real joy.

Key Project Details:

Project 1:

● Created a multi-criteria decision analysis (optimal location for a 20-25 hectare solar panel farm), incorporating the derivatives of our custom built DEM model

● Developed a DEM through free form experimentation with various interpolation techniques (Spline, IDW, Kriging, ANUDEM, Trend, etc.) and parameters

● DEM derivatives included hillshades, aspect, slope, and were also used to build 3D scene models

● In addition to aspect and slope, relation to power lines, and various distance restrictions were used as criteria

● Team Members:1 (name available upon request for faculty)

Project 2:

● Terrain and watershed processing in ArcHydro for a subwatershed in the Ganaraska Region Conservation Authority (GRCA), south of Lindsay, Ontario

● Explored the Arc Hydro tool set using a standard DEM (Fill Sinks, Flow Direction, Flow Accumulation, Drainage Line Processing, etc.)
