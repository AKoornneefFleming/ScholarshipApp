Since September 2022, I have had the distinguished pleasure of attending Fleming College's GIS Application Specialist program. Please enjoy this portfolio that highlights the involved courses, core competencies, key projects and assignments that I worked on, as well as my favourite moments.

An additional thanks to all the to the various team members that collaborated with me on various portfolio items, and the staff and faculty that made This program possible.

Course Highlights:

There are few things I enjoy more than stretching my creative muscles. However, never completing any formal graphic design or art based courses before, learning the visual frameworks involved in this course forced me to view the world around me from a brand new perspective. Experimenting with the Cindy Brewer's ColorBrewer 2.0 was particularly interesting, and has become a go-to reference when designing and selecting colour palettes.

Yearning to explore a northern Ontario community that I have never been to, I focused my technical labs on the city of Whitehorse, Yukon. My favourite of these products being a 1:50,000 topographic map.

I also enjoyed building my final project, in which I produced a 1:5,000 scale map illustrating the affect of structures damaged in the Nord-Kivu province of the Democratic Republic of Congo, resulting from the Mt. Nyrigagongo eruption in 2021. This was also a great experience as it allowed me to link my current studies back to my master's program studying Disaster & Emergency Management, when I did a deep dive on an earlier eruption of the same volcano.

A full size pdf of both the Yukon and the Mt. Nyrigagongo map are both available as full size PDFs in the GitHub repository.

Key Project Details:

● Produced a 1:5,000, 22 x 36' scale map illustrating the impacts of the 2021 Nyrigagongo, focused on the city perimeter of Goma in the Nord-Kivu province

● The intended audience for the map is emergency management services and local community members, which is why a large map scale was selected

● The focal element is the generalized outline of the lava flow approaching the city perimeter, intersected with the affected roads and building structures. These products were derived from data made available through UNITAR-UNOSAT and Sentinel-1 satellite imagery

● Due to the nature of the hazard, contour lines were emphasized in 5 meter detail

● A smaller scale inset map displays the entire extent of the lava flow over a hillshade, and helps contextualizes the spatial reference for the viewer

● This was an individual project, with direct feedback and guidance provided by my professor

Course Highlights:

Despite my dreams to become a video game programmer in eight grade, I had no practical experience with any programming languages before beginning this program. I now consider this programming course in it's entirety a highlight of the program. I still have to pinch myself when reflecting how much I have learned.

Nearly every new experience in Python trumped the last, starting with building my first 'Hello World' print statement, initiating my first for loop, reading and writing from a file, and manipulating ArcGIS project data using ArcPy.

The most interesting programs included various iterations of a hurricane wind speed calculator, drawing images through the use of object class methods, codes that solved basic trigonometric problems, and my team's self directed final project.

Key Project Details:

● Collaboratively designed and wrote a python program that calculates the minimum required floor height for an apartment hunter, to prevent full shade at various times of year, given various user inputs

● It also calculates statistical information about the amount of sunlight hours the user can expect to receive throughout the year

● The resulting information is packaged into a feature class, a shapefile, and added to the geodatabase of an ArcGIS Pro project

● This project incorporates ArcPy, as well as other python concepts learned over the semester

● Sunlight hour calculations are accurate within 30 minutes each day, with variance influenced by latitude. Optimal apartment height is based on the potential of receiving at least one hour of southern sunlight in a day. City inputs as an alternative to latitude and longitude are only available for major Canadian cities.

● Team members: Becca Carmichael, Yingjia Ye, one name permission pending

Spatial Analysis I

Grade achieved:

96%

Course Competencies:

● Compare and select the most appropriate data model and GIS analysis method for a problem

● Describe and perform basic GIS analysis with a variety of different data models

● Develop work flows to accomplish specific objectives and tasks at an introductory level

● Procure, interpret, transform, and integrate data from various sources, in different formats and coordinate systems, to solve spatial problems

● Effectively perform and complete a Spatial Analysis scenario, from data acquisition, to pre-processing, to analysis and presentation

● Use professional and technical language to describe industry use cases for spatial analysis

Course Highlights:

Few things feel as rewarding as successfully solving a problem. That is why each spatial analysis technique that I add to my professional toolbox makes me feel as though I'm walking a bit taller.

Site suitability analyses were particularly rewarding experiences (cottage site suitability, wildlife habitat suitability, etc.), but the standout experience of this course for me was building multi-criteria decision analysis to conduct a spatial based risk assessment. I have completed many risk assessments before. However, I have never had the opportunity to use GIS technology to incorporate spatial factors into into my criteria. Prior to beginning the program, this was the type of project that I envisioned myself working on.

This course was also my introduction to Esri's Model Builder functionality. Leveraging this toolset redefined how I use ArcGIS Pro, and has been beneficial in nearly every ArcGIS project I have completed since.

Key Project Details:

● Risk assessment of a proxy pipe network in Whitehorse, Yukon

● Analysis criteria included water distribution networks, transportation features, key social infrastructure, landuse, unstable environments, and attributes of the pipe network itself

● Input data was collected, analyzed and prepared over the course of the semester

● Risk was calculated as (likelihood * vulnerability * resilience * maximum consequence), whose values were calculated based on spatial assessments of the various analysis criteria.

● This was a professor-led assignment, applied to a unique study area

Both projects are highlights of the entire program, making me wish this course could go on for even longer; environmental modelling is riveting challenge.

Key Project Details:

Project 1:

● Developed a DEM through free form experimentation with various interpolation techniques (Spline, IDW, Kriging, ANUDEM, Trend, etc.) and their associated parameters

● DEM derivatives included hillshades, aspect, slope, and were also used to build 3D scene models

● In addition to aspect and slope, relation to power lines, and various distance restrictions were used as criteria
